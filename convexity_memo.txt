------------------------------------------------------------------------------
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Bull Bear currently parks ~100% of risk-off capital in money market
(SGOV or equivalent). Over the study period, CRDBX spent 252 of
744 trading days (34%) in risk-off mode, earning
approximately 3.0% annualized on those days.

This memo proposes replacing the risk-off allocation with:
  33% CAOS  (Alpha Architect Tail Risk -- convex put spreads on S&P)
  33% DBMF  (iMGP Managed Futures -- systematic trend following)
  34% SGOV  (iShares 0-3 Month Treasury -- cash anchor)

The thesis: CAOS and DBMF are designed to produce positive returns
during the exact market conditions that trigger our risk-off signal.
CAOS owns put spreads that appreciate when equities fall sharply.
DBMF runs trend-following strategies that profit from sustained moves
in any direction. Both are uncorrelated to equity beta in normal
markets but become positively convex during dislocations. Pairing them
with SGOV preserves the defensive intent while converting dead capital
into an active return source.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
METHODOLOGY
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Regime detection from CRDBX daily NAV (no lookahead bias):
  - Rolling 5-day beta of CRDBX vs S&P 500
  - Beta > 0.6 = risk-on  |  Beta < 0.3 = risk-off
  - Same-day confirmation: if |CRDBX change| < 0.05% while
    |S&P change| > 0.30%, classify as risk-off

Validation:
  - Detected risk-on beta:  1.49x (confirms VOO + futures)
  - Risk-on days:  490 (66%)
  - Risk-off days: 252 (34%)
  - Distinct risk-off periods: 60
  - Average duration: 4.2 days | Longest: 21 days

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
IMPLEMENTATION
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

No change to the risk-on sleeve or signal architecture. The only
modification is what the fund holds during risk-off periods:

  CURRENT:   100% SGOV / money market
  PROPOSED:  33% CAOS + 33% DBMF + 34% SGOV

Expense impact on the 33% risk-off capital:
  CAOS:  0.63% ER x 0.33 x 0.33 (risk-off %) =  0.07% on total fund
  DBMF:  0.85% ER x 0.33 x 0.33 (risk-off %) =  0.09% on total fund
  SGOV:  0.09% ER x 0.34 x 0.33 (risk-off %) =  0.01% on total fund
  Total incremental cost:                         0.17% on total fund
  Net of incremental return (46 bps):          +29 bps net pickup

Liquidity: All three ETFs trade >$5M daily volume. CAOS: $575M AUM.
DBMF: $1.4B AUM. No capacity constraint at Potomac's position sizes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RISKS AND LIMITATIONS
------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Limited backtest window (March 2023 - February 2026). This period
   did not include a severe bear market. CAOS and DBMF are designed
   to outperform during exactly those conditions, so the backtest
   likely understates the benefit.

2. Regime detection is approximate. We infer risk-on/risk-off from
   CRDBX NAV behavior, not from the actual proprietary signal. Minor
   misclassifications are possible but do not materially affect the
   aggregate statistics.

3. CAOS carries path-dependent risk: the put-spread structure decays
   in calm markets. However, at 33% of a 33% sleeve (effectively
   ~11% of risk-off capital), the drag is minimal and the convexity
   payoff during a true dislocation is asymmetrically favorable.
