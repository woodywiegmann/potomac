## THE ETHOS

Nelson Freeburg ran Formula Research out of Memphis, TN from the mid-1990s through the
mid-2000s. The publication served money managers across 26 countries. Every issue followed
the same discipline:

1. **Quantitative, rules-based** — Every strategy is defined by explicit formulas. No ambiguity,
   no "feel." If it can't be coded and backtested, it doesn't get published.

2. **Consensus of indicators** — "No single indicator could do the job in isolation. But when I
   treated the patterns in interaction, results proved compelling." The strongest models combine
   diverse, independent signals (breadth, sentiment, monetary, trend) into a structured composite.

3. **Out-of-sample validation is non-negotiable** — When Patrick Cunningham at Gardner-Lewis
   Asset Management offered extended Russell data back to 1979, Freeburg immediately
   retested everything on the unseen data. The findings held up. This is the gold standard.

4. **Drawdown is the risk metric, not volatility** — Every model is evaluated by maximum
   equity drawdown. A 15% return with 12% drawdown dominates a 20% return with 47% drawdown.
   Period.

5. **Real-world execution costs kill theoretical edge** — Freeburg repeatedly demonstrates
   that attractive annualized returns shrink dramatically once you account for switching
   logistics, bid-ask spreads, slippage, and time spent in cash. "Treating performance in terms
   of annualized returns offers a compressed, somewhat artificial portrait of price behavior."

6. **Intermarket analysis reveals lead-lag structure** — Markets are connected by predictable
   delays. Gold leads interest rates. Inflation leads unemployment. One sector's relative strength
   forecasts broad market direction. Finding and exploiting these lags is the core methodology.

7. **Discretion has a place** — Tom McClellan and Roger Kliminski "do not apply their methods
   mechanically. The managers can and do overrule the signals based on experience and judgment."
   Freeburg notes that "real-world returns surpass simulated results, especially in the area of
   risk control" when skilled practitioners exercise discretion.

## CORE CONCEPT 1: Russell 2000 Growth vs. Value — The Master Signal

### The Discovery

Roger Kliminski originally identified this pattern. Tom McClellan refined it. It is the single
most powerful finding in the entire Formula Research body of work.

**When Russell 2000 Growth leads Russell 2000 Value in relative strength, virtually the entire
stock market — every sector, every cap band — shows higher returns and lower risk. When
R2K Value leads, the opposite occurs with striking consistency.**

### The Measurement (Composite Relative Strength Ranking)

For each of the two sectors (R2K Growth, R2K Value):
1. Calculate the percentage change over the preceding **5 days**
2. Calculate the percentage change over the preceding **15 days**
3. Calculate the percentage change over the preceding **25 days**
4. Calculate the percentage change over the preceding **35 days**
5. **Average the four readings** into a single composite score
6. The sector with the **higher composite score** is the leader

### The Evidence

**S&P 500 Performance by Regime (1993-2003):**

| Condition | Annualized Return | Max Drawdown |
|-----------|------------------|-------------|
| R2K Growth dominant | **+17.4%** | **21%** |
| R2K Value dominant | +4.5% | 50% |
| S&P 500 buy-and-hold | +10.7% | 47% |

**All Four Russell Sectors (1995-2003):**

| Sector | R2K Growth Dominant | | R2K Value Dominant | |
|--------|-------|------|-------|------|
| | Return | DD | Return | DD |
| R1K Growth | +20.6% | 25% | -1.3% | 65% |
| R1K Value | +15.1% | 22% | +7.2% | 40% |
| R2K Growth | +40.4% | 20% | -20.4% | 78% |
| R2K Value | +24.3% | 17% | +1.7% | 44% |

**The Paradox:** R2K Value itself performs far better (+24.3%) when R2K Growth is dominant
than when R2K Value itself leads (+1.7%). This proves the signal is a market-wide regime
indicator, not a sector preference.

**NASDAQ Composite:**
- R2K Growth dominant: +41.9% annualized, 22% DD
- R2K Value dominant: -14.0% annualized, 80% DD

**15 Fidelity Select Sector Funds (1993-2003):**
- When R2K Growth dominant: **All 15 profitable**, average +34.4%, avg DD 26%
- When R2K Value dominant: **All 15 show losses**, average -7.5%, avg DD 66%

The individual sectors tested: Air Transport, Auto, Biotech, Brokerage, Cyclical, Devel.
Communications, Electronics, Energy Service, Industrial Equipment, Leisure, Multimedia,
Technology, Software, Telecom, Utility Growth. Not a single exception.

**34 Fidelity Select Fund Composite (1993-2003):**
- R2K Growth dominant: +21.4% annualized, 16% DD
- R2K Value dominant: +4.7% annualized, 42% DD

### The Interpretation

When growth-oriented small caps lead, it signals broad risk appetite and liquidity flowing into
speculative assets. The rising tide lifts all boats. When value small caps lead, it signals
defensive positioning and risk aversion — the market is in "damage mode" even when it
doesn't look like it on the surface.

## CORE CONCEPT 2: The Four-Sector Switching Strategy

### The Method

Track four Russell sub-indices daily: **R1K Growth, R1K Value, R2K Growth, R2K Value.**

For each, compute the composite relative strength score (average of 5/15/25/35-day percent
changes). Switch to the sector with the highest score. Stay there until another sector claims
the top spot.

### Daily Version Results (1993-2003)

| Strategy | Annual Return | Max Drawdown |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| Four-sector switching | **20.1%** | 33% |
| R1K Growth buy-hold | 9.1% | 66% |
| R1K Value buy-hold | 11.3% | — |
| R2K Growth buy-hold | 6.0% | 68% |
| R2K Value buy-hold | 12.7% | 44% |
| S&P 500 buy-hold | 10.7% | 47% |

### Monthly Variant (Patrick Cunningham, 1980-2003)

Use **3-month, 6-month, and 12-month** rates of change instead. Switch monthly.

| Strategy | Annual Return | Growth of $10,000 |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Monthly switching | **16.8%** | **$408,000** |
| S&P 500 buy-hold | 13.2% | $191,000 |

### Key Insight

Switching between just R1K and R2K (the parent indices) returned only 11.8% — barely
beating the S&P. Breaking the data into four discrete growth/value sub-groups is what
unlocks the edge. The additional granularity reveals information invisible in the parent indices.

## CORE CONCEPT 3: The Three-Component Switch Fund Model

**This is the culminating, implementable trading system. It beat every other Formula Research
model in both return and risk.**

### The Problem It Solves

Using any single signal (R2K Growth/Value, NASDAQ RS, or OEX RS) alone produced
theoretical annualized returns that looked great but translated into mediocre real-world
switch fund performance once you accounted for time spent in cash. The R2K signal alone
yielded 10.7% switching the S&P — identical to buy-and-hold.

Freeburg: "No single indicator could do the job in isolation. But when I treated the patterns
in interaction, results proved compelling."

### The Three Components

1. **NASDAQ / S&P 500 Relative Strength**
   - Compute: NASDAQ Composite / S&P 500 (daily ratio)
   - Compare to its **50-day simple moving average**
   - Ratio above 50-day SMA → **BULLISH**
   - Ratio below 50-day SMA → **BEARISH**

2. **OEX / S&P 500 Relative Strength**
   - Compute: S&P 100 (OEX) / S&P 500 (daily ratio)
   - Compare to its **50-day simple moving average**
   - Ratio above 50-day SMA → **BULLISH**
   - Ratio below 50-day SMA → **BEARISH**

3. **Russell 2000 Growth vs. Value Composite**
   - Use the standard composite ranking formula (average of 5/15/25/35-day ROC)
   - R2K Growth has higher composite score → **BULLISH**
   - R2K Value has higher composite score → **BEARISH**

### The Trading Rules

- **All three BULLISH** → Buy S&P 500 index fund (SPY, VFINX, VOO)
- **All three BEARISH** → Exit to money market / treasuries
- **Mixed** → Hold current position (no new action)

### Results (mid-1993 to 2003)

| Metric | Three-Component Model | S&P 500 Buy-and-Hold |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|
| Annual return | **15.3%** | 10.7% |
| Max drawdown | **11.7%** | 47% |
| Win rate | **82%** (14/17 trades) | — |

**This beat every Formula Research stock market timing model over the same timeframe,
in both return AND risk.**

## CORE CONCEPT 4: The Mekros Leveraged Russell 2000 Strategy

### The Idea

Trade the Rydex Mekros fund (RYMKX), a 1.5x leveraged Russell 2000 index fund, using
the R2K Growth/Value signal.

### Results

| Strategy | Annual Return | Max Drawdown |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Long-only (buy when R2K Growth leads, cash when Value leads) | **22.8%** | 25% |
| Long-and-short | **26.8%** | 39% |
| Short-only (short when R2K Value leads, cash when Growth leads) | **8.4%** | 33% |
| Reversed logic (long Value, short Growth) | **-28.9%** | 98% |
| Mekros buy-and-hold | 8.5% | 67% |
| S&P 500 buy-and-hold | 10.7% | 47% |

### The Short-Selling Breakthrough

Generating 8.4% annually from the **short side only** of an equity strategy is exceptional.
Freeburg: "If you have ever tried to develop a stock market timing model with the option to
go short, and if you tested that model going back many decades, you know how hard it is to
capitalize on periodic corrections in the market." The R2K Growth/Value signal makes short
selling "feasible over a wider range of applications."

## CORE CONCEPT 5: Intermarket Leading Indicators

### Gold → Short-Term Interest Rates (15-month lead)

Gold price trends are echoed in 90-day T-bill rates approximately 15 months later. McClellan
used this to correctly forecast the surge in T-bill rates.

### CPI Inflation → Unemployment (2-year lead)

The inflation rate leads the unemployment rate by approximately two years. "The fit is not
perfect but the overall symmetry is impressive."

### PMI → Fed Rate Moves

- PMI rises above **56** → expect initial Fed rate hike
- PMI drops below **50** → expect Fed easing
- Simple PMI crossover model (above/below 50 level):
  - **84% accuracy** (49 of 58 calls correct since 1950)
  - Captured **7,311 basis points** vs 12 bps for buy-and-hold
  - "Perhaps the most improbably accurate forecasting tool"

### Copper COT → Bond Prices

When commercial copper futures traders (Commitment of Traders data) get heavily net long
or short, bond prices tend to move in the opposite direction.

### The Goldollar Index (Gold x U.S. Dollar Index)

Isolates gold's trend from currency effects. On a long-term basis, trends in gold are often
foreshadowed months in advance by the Goldollar Index.

**Trading system:**
- Long when Comex gold < 3-day SMA AND Goldollar > 3-day SMA
- Short when gold > 3-day SMA AND Goldollar < 3-day SMA
- Exit long on stop at lowest low of past 15 days
- Exit short on stop at highest high of past 15 days
- Since 1975: **$161,540/contract** in gold futures, **$317,959/contract** in silver
- Drawdown: $15,180 (gold), $23,000 (silver)

### Gold Lease Rate Spread

Plot the difference between 1-month and 12-month gold lease rates (London Bullion Market
Association). McClellan finds the spread "revealing" for precious metals timing.

## CORE CONCEPT 6: Sector Leadership as Regime Indicator

### NASDAQ Relative Strength (since 1971)

| Condition | S&P 500 Annualized |
|-----------|-------------------|
| NASDAQ leads (ratio > 44-day EMA) | **14.6%** |
| NASDAQ lags | 7.1% |
| Buy-and-hold | 11.1% |

Gerald Appel first identified this tendency. McClellan refined it using the NASDAQ/NYSE
Composite ratio vs. its 44-day exponential moving average.

### OEX (S&P 100) Relative Strength (since 1976)

| Condition | S&P 500 Annualized |
|-----------|-------------------|
| OEX leads (ratio > MA) | **15.1%** |
| OEX lags | 10.9% |
| Buy-and-hold | 12.5% |

McClellan's original observation: "When the S&P 100 is stronger than the S&P 500, stocks
in general tend to advance."

### Combined (since 1976)

| Condition | S&P 500 Annualized |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Both NASDAQ and OEX positive | **16.2%** |
| Both negative | 7.3% |

**"When the OEX and NASDAQ are dominant, you want to be quick to buy and slow to sell.
When both sectors are weak, you want to be slow to buy and quick to sell."**

## REFERENCED MODELS (Sidebar Features)

### Zweig Bond Timing Model (Featured March 1993)

Originally from Martin Zweig's 1987 book *Winning with the New IRAs*, developed by Ned
Davis Research. Combines trend-following elements with fundamental inputs.

- Since 1970: **12.9% annually** vs 10.2% for DJ Corporate Bond Index
- Max drawdown: **4%** vs 21% for buy-and-hold
- Freeburg added refinements; all versions continued performing in real time
- Applied successfully to Fidelity Investment Grade Bond fund (FBNDX)

### K-Ratio Gold Timing Model (Featured October 1994)

Invented by Jay Kaeppel. Compares gold stocks to gold bullion. Uses adaptive bands:
countertrend channel picks tops/bottoms, trend-following channel captures impulse.

- Since 1976: **17.7% annually**, <15% drawdown
- Gold buy-and-hold: 3.84% annually, 70% drawdown

### Hendrickson Stock Market Timing Model (Featured January 1995)

Simple but effective method by Harland Hendrickson.

- Since 1970: **13.0% annually** vs 11.1% for S&P 500
- Max drawdown: **13.3%** vs 46% for S&P
- **10/10 buy signals profitable**, including all three trades since publication

## DIRECT APPLICATION TO PFM / POTOMAC

### The Potomac Defensive Bull Fund IS a Freeburg-Style Switch Strategy

The commission report shows Potomac rotating between full S&P 500 exposure (Vanguard S&P,
iShares S&P, E-mini futures) and treasuries (BBLX 6M UST ETF). This is structurally
identical to the Three-Component Switch Fund Model: invest in S&P when conditions are
bullish, exit to safety when bearish.

### Five Questions for Dan

1. **What signal drives the Potomac rotation?** Is it the R2K Growth/Value regime, the
   NASDAQ/OEX relative strength, or some other indicator set? Understanding the signal
   architecture is prerequisite to improving it.

2. **Is the rotation frequency justified?** The commission data shows multiple full rotations
   per month (~$99K/20 days). The Three-Component Model's requirement for all-three-bullish
   or all-three-bearish consensus specifically reduces whipsaws. If Potomac is using a
   single-signal trigger, adding consensus requirements could reduce turnover by 30-50%
   while improving both returns and drawdown.

3. **Can we monitor the signals in real-time?** The `freeburg_signals.py` engine computes
   all three components daily using free Yahoo Finance data. Running it produces an immediate
   read on the current regime (FULL_BULL / FULL_BEAR / MIXED) with actionable context.

4. **Is the "defensive" allocation optimal?** Freeburg used money market / commercial paper
   as the defensive instrument. Potomac uses UST ETFs. During certain rate environments,
   one may outperform the other. The Zweig Bond Timing Model could inform whether to
   be in short-duration treasuries vs longer-duration during defensive periods.

5. **Should we add a short component?** The Mekros strategy showed that the R2K Growth/Value
   signal generates 8.4% annually from the short side alone — exceptional for a short-only
   equity strategy. For a fund with futures capabilities (which Potomac clearly has via E-mini
   contracts), a short overlay during R2K Value dominance could turn defensive periods into
   profit centers rather than just drawdown avoidance.
